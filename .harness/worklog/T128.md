Added `dedupeAlbumResults(results)` to `src/lib/lastfm.js` — a pure function that collapses
near-identical Last.fm album search results (e.g. the "808s and Heartbreaks" family of
crowd-tagged duplicates) into one representative per normalized group:
- Normalizes title/artist: lowercase, strips a leading artist-name prefix from the title,
  strips parenthetical/trailing feat/ft qualifiers, strips punctuation (`&`/`.`/`,`/`'`),
  folds `&`/`and`, collapses whitespace, and folds trivial trailing-`s` plural/singular
  variants (needed to fold "808s & Heartbreak" in with "...Heartbreaks").
- Within a group, prefers the first result with a non-empty `mbid`; otherwise keeps the
  first result seen (preserving Last.fm's relevance order).
- Preserves first-appearance order of survivors; does not mutate the input array.

Wired into `src/pages/api/lastfm/album-search.js`: the handler now returns
`dedupeAlbumResults(raw.map(mapAlbumSearchResult))` instead of the raw mapped array.
`mapAlbumSearchResult`'s per-result shape is unchanged.

Added tests in `src/lib/lastfm.test.js` covering: the 4-item "808s and Heartbreaks" family
collapsing to 1 result, mbid-bearing duplicates winning over mbid-less ones in the same
group, distinct albums/artists surviving untouched, first-appearance order being preserved,
and the input array not being mutated.
