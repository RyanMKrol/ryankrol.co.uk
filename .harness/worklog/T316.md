# T316 — 2026-07-07

## What
Reverted the regression introduced by T314: `.project-card-archived-tag` had `top`/`left`
changed from `0` to `var(--border-width)`, on the mistaken reasoning that this "matches the
effective behaviour it already had." T314's own worklog measurements actually show the
opposite: with `top: 0; left: 0`, an absolutely-positioned child resolves against its
positioned ancestor's *padding box* (whose origin is the inner face of the ancestor's
border), not the border-box origin `getBoundingClientRect()` reports for the card — so
`top: 0; left: 0` was already flush against the inside of the border with zero extra gap.
Adding an explicit `var(--border-width)` stacked a second, redundant offset, leaving a
visible ~2px white gap along the straight top/left edges.

Changed `src/styles/globals.css` `.project-card-archived-tag`:
- `top: var(--border-width)` → `top: 0`
- `left: var(--border-width)` → `left: 0`

Left `border-top-left-radius: calc(var(--radius-card) - var(--border-width))` and
`border-bottom-right-radius: var(--radius-card)` untouched — those were T314's correct fix
for the corner-radius seam and are unrelated to this bug.
