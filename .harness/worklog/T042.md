# T042 worklog

## What I did

Wrote `.harness/designs/T042-ui-rewrite-briefs.md` containing three structurally
distinct full-site UI/IA rewrite design briefs, per the task spec:

1. **Dense Data-Table Console** — left sidebar nav, everything as sortable tables,
   slide-in right-hand detail panel, weight/color-driven typographic hierarchy (no
   size contrast).
2. **Editorial Card Magazine** — masthead top nav, hero + masonry card grid, full-page
   article-style detail/edit, serif/sans display+body pairing with dramatic size
   contrast.
3. **Minimal Linear Feed** — slim utility bar only (no persistent nav), single linear
   date-gutter timeline (no grid at all), inline-expand detail/edit (no separate
   pages), single-size/single-family typography with spacing-driven hierarchy only.

Each brief covers: name/identity, navigation paradigm, density approach, list-view
description, detail/form-view description, and typography/hierarchy approach — the six
required elements. The three were deliberately designed to sit at three different
extremes (grid/table vs. uneven masonry vs. no-grid-at-all; weight-driven vs.
size+family-driven vs. spacing-only hierarchy; persistent sidebar vs. masthead vs. no
persistent nav) so they aren't just re-skins of one layout.

Researched current site first by reading root `CLAUDE.md`'s "What this project is"
section and skimming `src/pages/index.js`, `src/pages/reviews/movies/index.js`,
`src/pages/vinyl/index.js`, `src/pages/workouts/index.js` to ground the contrast in
what actually exists today (centered container, `home-grid` card grid, `ReviewCard`/
`WorkoutCard` list pages with a `SortButtons` row, letter-grouped vinyl).
